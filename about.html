About Us

Building a More Transparent Future

We're a team of environmental scientists, engineers, and open-source advocates working to democratize environmental monitoring worldwide.

Our Story

OpenSense began in 2018 when a group of hydrologists and engineers realized that the $10,000+ price tag on commercial environmental sensors was preventing communities from accessing the data they needed to address water challenges.

We started by reverse-engineering expensive sensors and rebuilding them with off-the-shelf components. What began as a weekend project in a university lab has grown into a global network of researchers, practitioners, and communities using open-source technology to monitor rivers, watersheds, and ecosystems.

Today, our sensors are deployed across six continents, from the Amazon rainforest to the Himalayas, from California vineyards to Kenyan irrigation systems. We've proven that accessible technology doesn't mean compromising on quality ‚Äî our sensors consistently match the performance of equipment costing 20-50 times more.

But OpenSense is more than just sensors. It's a movement toward transparent, accessible environmental science. We believe that the communities most affected by environmental challenges should have the tools to understand and address them.
